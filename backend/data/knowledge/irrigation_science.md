# Irrigation Science for Tomato Cultivation

## Crop Water Requirements

### Growth Stage Water Needs
| Growth Stage | Duration (days) | Daily Water Need (mm/day) | Critical Period |
|-------------|-----------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Transplanting | 1-10 | 2-3 | Establishment critical |
| Vegetative | 11-35 | 3-5 | Root development |
| Flowering | 36-60 | 5-7 | Most critical — water stress causes flower drop |
| Fruit Development | 61-85 | 6-8 | Highest demand — affects fruit size |
| Ripening | 86-110 | 4-5 | Reduce to improve quality and prevent cracking |

### Total Seasonal Water Requirement
- **Tomato**: 400-600 mm total (depending on climate, variety, and season)
- **Peak demand**: 7-8 mm/day during fruit development in hot conditions

## Irrigation Methods Comparison

### Drip Irrigation (Recommended)
- **Water use efficiency**: 90-95%
- **Yield improvement**: 20-30% over flood irrigation
- **Spacing**: Laterals 1.5m apart, drippers every 30-40cm, 2-4 LPH
- **Advantages**: Precise water delivery, reduced disease (no foliar wetting), fertigation capability
- **Schedule**: 1-2 hours daily during peak demand, based on ET₀

### Sprinkler Irrigation
- **Water use efficiency**: 70-80%
- **Caution**: Avoid during flowering (can dislodge pollen) and when fungal disease pressure is high
- **Best use**: Pre-transplant soil preparation, frost protection

### Flood/Furrow Irrigation
- **Water use efficiency**: 40-60%
- **Schedule**: Every 5-7 days in dry season, adjust for rainfall
- **Issues**: Over-watering risk, disease promotion, nutrient leaching

## ET₀-Based Irrigation Scheduling

### Reference Evapotranspiration (ET₀)
Calculated using Penman-Monteith equation considering: temperature, humidity, wind speed, solar radiation.

### Crop Coefficient (Kc)
| Growth Stage | Kc Value |
|-------------|----------|
| Initial | 0.4-0.5 |
| Development | 0.7-0.8 |
| Mid-season | 1.05-1.15 |
| Late season | 0.8-0.9 |

**Crop Water Requirement (ETc)** = ET₀ × Kc

## Soil Moisture Monitoring

### Field Capacity and Wilting Point
| Soil Type | Field Capacity (%) | Wilting Point (%) | Available Water (mm/m) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Sandy | 10-15 | 4-6 | 60-90 |
| Sandy Loam | 15-22 | 6-10 | 90-120 |
| Loam | 22-30 | 10-15 | 120-150 |
| Clay Loam | 28-35 | 14-20 | 140-150 |
| Clay | 35-45 | 20-25 | 150-200 |

### Management Allowable Depletion (MAD)
- Tomatoes: 40-50% of available water
- Irrigate when soil moisture drops below (Field Capacity - MAD)
- More frequent, lighter irrigations are preferred over infrequent heavy ones

## Deficit Irrigation Strategies
- **Regulated Deficit Irrigation (RDI)**: Deliberately stress during vegetative stage to control vigor, then full irrigation during flowering/fruiting
- Can save 20-30% water with minimal yield impact if managed correctly
- **Not recommended** during flowering and early fruit development stages
