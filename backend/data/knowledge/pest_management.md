# Integrated Pest Management for Tomatoes

## Common Tomato Pests

### 1. Whiteflies (Bemisia tabaci)
- **Biology**: Tiny white-winged insects, 1-2mm. Complete lifecycle 21-30 days. Thrive in warm, dry conditions (25-30°C).
- **Damage**: Sap sucking causes leaf yellowing, honeydew secretion leads to sooty mold. Vector for Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV).
- **Economic Threshold**: 5-10 adults per leaf on upper canopy.
- **IPM Protocol**:
  - **Biological**: Release Encarsia formosa parasitoid wasps (1-3 per m²). Use Beauveria bassiana biopesticide.
  - **Cultural**: Yellow sticky traps (1 per 10m²). Remove infested lower leaves. Reflective mulch.
  - **Chemical (last resort)**: Spiromesifen 22.9% SC @ 0.7ml/L. Rotate with Diafenthiuron 50% WP @ 1g/L.
  - **Safety interval**: 7 days before harvest.

### 2. Fruit Borer (Helicoverpa armigera)
- **Biology**: Moth lays eggs on flowers/young fruits. Larvae bore into fruits within 24h of hatching.
- **Damage**: Direct fruit damage, 20-50% yield loss in severe infestations.
- **Economic Threshold**: 1-2 larvae per plant or 5% fruit damage.
- **IPM Protocol**:
  - **Biological**: Trichogramma egg parasitoids (1 lakh/acre, 6 releases). Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) spray @ 1g/L.
  - **Cultural**: Pheromone traps (5/acre) for monitoring. Bird perches (20/acre). Hand-pick visible larvae.
  - **Chemical**: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG @ 0.4g/L. Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC @ 0.3ml/L.
  - **Safety interval**: 5-7 days.

### 3. Aphids (Myzus persicae, Aphis gossypii)
- **Biology**: Small soft-bodied insects, rapid reproduction. Populations explode in moderate temperatures (15-25°C).
- **Damage**: Sap sucking, leaf curling, virus transmission (CMV, PVY).
- **Economic Threshold**: 20-30 aphids per leaf.
- **IPM Protocol**:
  - **Biological**: Ladybird beetles, lacewings, Aphidius colemani parasitoids.
  - **Cultural**: Strong water spray to dislodge. Neem oil 0.3% (1500 ppm azadirachtin).
  - **Chemical**: Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 0.3ml/L (avoid during flowering — toxic to pollinators).

### 4. Leaf Miner (Liriomyza trifolii)
- **Biology**: Small fly whose larvae mine serpentine tunnels through leaf tissue.
- **Damage**: Reduces photosynthetic area, weakens plants, entry point for pathogens.
- **Economic Threshold**: 5 mines per leaf or 20% leaves affected.
- **IPM Protocol**:
  - **Biological**: Diglyphus isaea parasitoid. Neem-based products.
  - **Cultural**: Remove and destroy affected leaves. Yellow sticky traps.
  - **Chemical**: Cyromazine 75% WP @ 0.4g/L (insect growth regulator, lower environmental impact).

## Seasonal Pest Pressure Calendar
| Season | Primary Pests | Risk Level |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Kharif (Jun-Oct) | Fruit borer, whitefly | High |
| Rabi (Oct-Feb) | Aphids, leaf miner | Moderate |
| Summer (Mar-Jun) | Whitefly, mites | High |

## Pesticide Resistance Management
- Rotate chemical classes every 2-3 applications
- Never use the same active ingredient consecutively
- Follow label rates precisely — sub-lethal doses accelerate resistance
- Maintain refuge areas for natural enemy populations
